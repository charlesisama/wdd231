Discover Port Harcourt

Pleasure part

Port Harcourt Pleasure Park is the city’s leading family-oriented recreation and entertainment hub, located along the major Aba Road between Bori Camp and the Air Force Base. It is managed by Julius Berger and is known for its high security and well-maintained green spaces.

Port Harcourt Mall

Port Harcourt Mall (commonly known as SPAR Mall) is the city's premier one-stop retail and lifestyle destination. Located in the heart of the city next to the Government House, it features a mix of international brands, a large hypermarket, and a popular food court.

Isaac Boro Park

Isaac Boro Park is a popular public park and outdoor recreation area located in the Old GRA neighborhood of Port Harcourt, directly opposite the Mile One Flyover. Established in 1970, it serves as both a historical monument and a peaceful green escape from the city’s bustle.

Rivers Museum

TransAmadi Industria Area

The Trans Amadi Industrial Layout is a 1,000-hectare (2,500-acre) zone that serves as the industrial and commercial core of Port Harcourt. It is widely regarded as the center of Nigeria's oil business, housing numerous multinational corporations and large-scale manufacturing plants

Bonny Island

Bonny Island, located at the southern tip of Rivers State, is Nigeria’s primary hub for liquefied natural gas and is often cited as the "richest island" in the country due to its significant contribution to the national GDP. Recent Milestone: Driving to Bonny For the first time in history, you can now reach Bonny Island by road. Bodo-Bonny Road Status: As of April 2026, the 39km road and bridge network is officially open for light vehicles (cars and SUVs) between 7 AM and 7 PM. Completion: While currently motorable, the final "Phase II" finishing touches are targeted for completion in 100% by the end of March 2026

University of Port Harcourt

The University of Port Harcourt (UNIPORT), established in 1975, is a prestigious public research university located in Rivers State, Nigeria. It is currently ranked among the top institutions in the country and operates across three main campuses: University Park (Abuja), Choba, and Aluu.

Indorama Petrochemicals

Eleme Petrochemicals Limited, now officially known as Indorama Eleme Petrochemicals Limited (IEPL), is a major petrochemical complex located on the outskirts of Port Harcourt, Rivers State. Originally established in 1990 as a subsidiary of the Nigerian National Petroleum Corporation (NNPC), it was privatised in 2006 with the Indorama Corporation emerging as the core investor.
